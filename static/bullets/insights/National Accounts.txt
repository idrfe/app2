GDP growth will dip well beneath the ten-year pre-pandemic average of
3.7% in 2023 on high inflation and the effects of past hikes to the policy
rate, which is now above the neutral rate of interest according to the
Central Bank. A softer global economy will further hit growth via exports.
Key factors to monitor include commodity prices and the president’s
policy agenda. FocusEconomics panelists project GDP to grow 1.2% in
2023, which is unchanged from last month’s forecast. For 2024, panelists
see GDP growth at 2.2%.
