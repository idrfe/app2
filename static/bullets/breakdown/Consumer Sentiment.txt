According to a survey elaborated by Fedesarrollo, consumer sentiment fell to
minus 28.5 in March from February’s minus 27.8. As such, the index fell even
further below the 0-point threshold, pointing to worsening pessimism among
consumers.
Consumers became more pessimistic about their financial situations and buying
intentions, driving the downturn in the index in March. That said, consumers
became more optimistic about their financial situations 12 months ahead and
less pessimistic about the economy 12 months ahead.
LatinFocus Consensus Forecast participants see private consumption expanding
0.7% in 2023, which is unchanged from last month’s forecast. In 2024, private
consumption growth is seen at 2.2%.
